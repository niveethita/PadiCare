Chilli plants cultivated in the Malay landscape typically take 5 months to mature. Chilli crop cultivation generally takes place from early January to May. The soil preparation for sowing chilli seeds begins in January. The harvesting of the chilli crops takes place in May. A land area of 1 acre can accommodate 4000 chilli trees. The chilli harvest yields an average of 2 kg to 3 kg per tree for every season. A farmer can generate RM 40000.00 from 1 acre of chilli cultivation over a 5 month period.

The harvested rock melons are typically sold for RM 3.00 each. The rock melon harvest generates RM 16500.00 per 1 acre for each season. Cultivating rock melon 2 times every 1 year on 1 acre of land provides a return of RM 33000.00 every 1 year.

Local farmers in the Malay landscape face income challenges due to rising farming costs and insufficient land size. Many farming families operate on a small land size of only 3.9 hectares per family. The downward trend in global crude palm oil prices severely impacts the earnings of local farmers. Some palm oil farmers report earning only RM 280.00 per month due to these declining commodity prices. Rubber farmers also face economic difficulties because the price of rubber dropped sharply to between RM 2.00 per kg and RM 2.40 per kg. These drops in commodity prices force local farmers to switch to cultivating cash crops like bananas and pineapples as a livelihood initiative.
